I am a **Ph.D. researcher in Energy Engineering** at [ICARE (CNRS)](https://icare.cnrs.fr/) in Orléans, France, working at the intersection of **combustion science, hydrogen energy, and optical diagnostics**. My doctoral research develops non-intrusive sensing techniques for hydrogen flames, combining high-fidelity numerical modelling with advanced experimental diagnostics.

My thesis, *"Assessment of OH\* as a flame marker in premixed H₂–air flames,"* investigates the relationship between OH\* radical chemiluminescence and heat-release rate to validate OH\* as a reliable flame-front marker. By correlating experimental diagnostic data with detailed chemical-kinetics modelling, I identify the chemical pathways governing OH\* formation under varying conditions — work that supports the development of robust, non-intrusive sensors for clean hydrogen combustion.

## Background

Before my doctorate, I spent several years applying combustion and process engineering in industry. As an **R&D Combustion Engineer at Fives European Combustion Centre** (Italy), I developed low-NOx and ultra-low-NOx burners — planning test campaigns, running HAZOP and risk analyses, and characterising emissions across a range of fuels including steam-diluted and CO₂-injected conditions. Earlier, as an **Assistant Manager (Technical Services) at Zuari Agro Chemical Limited** (India), I supported ammonia, urea, power, and water-treatment plants through material and energy balances, equipment sizing, bottleneck analysis, and energy audits.

I hold an **M.Sc. in Energy Engineering** from Politecnico di Milano and a **B.E. in Chemical Engineering** from Dayananda Sagar College of Engineering, and I work across the full chain from experiment to simulation.
